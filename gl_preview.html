OpenGL/WebGL Projects

Raytracing Effects

With this demo, I explored the mechanics behind rendering high-quality frames using RayTracing. Using Web-GL I recreated the vector math behind raytracing. I worked with complex Transluscent and Refracting surfaces, and even Procedural, mathematically derived materials for textured surfaces. In addition, I experimented with varying levels of Anti-Aliasing, as well as Jittering to reduce jagged lines. With this demo, you can test and compare effects of these various optimization settings yourself.

Particle Systems

I found Particle Systems to be one of the most exciting tools in a Technical Artist's toolkit. While I have created any particle systems in editors such as Unity, I sought to understand the underlaying structure behind particles. Here, I created systems replicating 3D Cloth Physics, Boids Avoidence, and even Coulomb's Law. While doing so, I experiemented with various physics solvers ranging from Midpoint, Implicit, to AdamsBashforth solvers . You can compare the differences yourself in this demo!

Vertex Buffers and Scene Graphs

In this project, I replicated the vertex and index buffers that all vertex data must travel through in order to render. I applied position and color data to each vertex, creating a unique "metallic" shade. I also designed a rudimentary scene-graph system that allowed for many "joints", modifying the transforms upon each step. In addition, I implemented a Quaternion system for rotation.

Blinn-Phong Shaders

Like Particle systems, I found shaders to be an unbelievably useful tool that made what was previously impossible, possible. While I have used many "Shadergraph" tools to create various artistic shaders, I also hoped to understand how to create new shaders using GLSL. Here, I replicated and compared many common lighting shaders, from old Gourand Shaders, to newer Blinn-phong shaders. I also coded various material properties to the surfaces for unique shiny effects.
